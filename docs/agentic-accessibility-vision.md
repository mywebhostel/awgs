# Agentic Accessibility Vision

Status: Canonical initiative vision document

## Definition

Agentic Accessibility is the principle that individuals and organizations should be able to interact with digital systems through trusted autonomous agents acting on their behalf.

## Hierarchy

```text
Agentic Accessibility Initiative
  Standards
    AWGS - Agentic Web Governance Standard
```

Agentic Accessibility is the broader public-interest vision. AWGS is the first concrete standard supporting that vision.

## Context

The web is moving through several overlapping modes of interaction:

- Human Web: people directly navigate websites, forms, and services.
- Search Web: search engines organize public information and discovery.
- Accessible Web: accessibility standards and universal design practices improve participation for people using diverse abilities, tools, and contexts.
- Agentic Web: trusted autonomous agents may increasingly discover, interpret, negotiate with, and act across digital services for people and organizations.

Agentic Accessibility asks how digital systems can support this delegated interaction responsibly.

## Governance Need

Trusted digital representatives need:

- Discoverability
- Identity
- Authorization
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Trust
- Privacy
- Safety
- Human oversight

These capabilities help services understand what an agent can do, who it represents, what authority it has, what risks exist, and how humans can review or intervene when needed.

## Relationship to Accessibility Standards

Agentic Accessibility is analogous to Universal Design and WCAG in the sense that it asks how digital systems can be made more broadly usable through shared design principles and public standards.

This analogy should not be read as a legal claim or as a statement that existing accessibility law already covers agent-mediated interaction.

## Relationship to AWGS

AWGS is the first concrete standard under the Agentic Accessibility Initiative. It supports the vision by defining governance, interoperability, transparency, accountability, trust, privacy, safety, and human oversight requirements for agentic digital services.

AWGS remains vendor-neutral and implementation-independent.
