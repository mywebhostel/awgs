Standards under the Agentic Accessibility Initiative

AWGS 1.0 is the first concrete standard. Future areas are exploratory until they are drafted, reviewed, and published.

Published Public Draft

AWGS 1.0

The Agentic Web Governance Standard is the first standard developed under the broader Agentic Accessibility vision. It defines vendor-neutral governance, interoperability, transparency, accountability, trust, privacy, safety, and oversight expectations for agentic digital services.

Future standard areas

These topics are research directions and planning areas. They are not finalized requirements or certification criteria.

Exploratory / Future Work

Agent Capability Manifest

A possible format for describing agent-accessible capabilities, limits, service boundaries, and machine-readable policies.

Exploratory / Future Work

Agent Identity Profile

A possible profile for identifying agents, delegated authority, service identities, credentials, and trusted representatives.

Exploratory / Future Work

Agent Trust Metadata

A possible metadata model for provenance, authenticity, reliability signals, and trust context in agentic interactions.

Exploratory / Future Work

Agent Governance Profiles

Possible implementation profiles for governance domains, risk posture, oversight expectations, and assurance evidence.

Exploratory / Future Work

Agent-to-Agent Governance

Future governance models for interactions involving multiple agents, delegated tasks, and shared accountability.

Exploratory / Future Work

Continuous Compliance Models

Future approaches for monitoring compliance signals, evidence freshness, and maturity evidence over time.
