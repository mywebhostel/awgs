Agentic Accessibility Initiative

AWGS is the first standard developed under the broader Agentic Accessibility vision. It remains vendor-neutral, implementation-independent, and focused on trustworthy agentic digital services.

Read the vision
Governance Domains

Domains

AWGS requirements are organized into nine domains that help teams plan implementation controls and assessment evidence.

Discoverability

D1

Machine-readable discovery of services and capabilities.

  • Capability descriptions
  • Service catalogs
  • Structured metadata
  • Machine-readable documentation

Interoperability

D2

Communication and integration between agents and systems.

  • Service interfaces
  • Standardized interaction mechanisms
  • Capability exchange
  • Protocol compatibility

Identity

D3

Identifying agents and services.

  • Agent identity
  • Service identity
  • Credential management
  • Delegated identity

Authorization

D4

Permissions and delegated authority.

  • Access scopes
  • Least privilege
  • Delegated permissions
  • Purpose-based access

Transparency

D5

Explainability and visibility of actions.

  • Decision explanations
  • Policy explanations
  • Action explanations

Accountability

D6

Traceability and auditability.

  • Audit logs
  • Event records
  • Provenance tracking
  • Accountability records

Trust

D7

Establishing confidence in interactions.

  • Verification mechanisms
  • Reputation indicators
  • Authenticity validation
  • Source trustworthiness

Privacy

D8

Responsible handling of information.

  • Consent
  • Data minimization
  • Purpose limitation
  • Privacy controls

Safety

D9

Operational safeguards.

  • Risk assessment
  • Human approval workflows
  • Escalation mechanisms
  • Rate limiting
  • Operational constraints
