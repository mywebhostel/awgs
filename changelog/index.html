Standards and Guidelines Change Log

This public change log records changes to the Agentic Accessibility vision, AWGS standard, and supporting guidelines. It does not record website implementation, hosting, build, or repository tooling changes.

2026-06-07

Agentic Accessibility Initiative

  • Repositioned the site as the public home for the broader Agentic Accessibility Initiative.
  • Defined Agentic Accessibility as the principle that individuals and organizations should be able to interact with digital systems through trusted autonomous agents acting on their behalf.
  • Clarified that AWGS is the first concrete standard under the broader Agentic Accessibility vision.
  • Added initiative-level principles for delegated access, machine-readable participation, transparent representation, and accountable automation.
  • Added the Agentic Accessibility Manifesto as an informative vision document.

AWGS 1.0 Foundation Specification

  • Published the AWGS 1.0 Foundation Specification as a Public Draft Standard.
  • Established the AWGS purpose and vision for trustworthy interactions between autonomous agents and digital services.
  • Defined vendor neutrality requirements for AWGS compliance.
  • Defined the AWGS 1.0 scope, including in-scope and out-of-scope system categories.
  • Added eight core principles: discoverability, interoperability, transparency, accountability, trust, privacy, safety, and human oversight.
  • Added nine governance domains: discoverability, interoperability, identity, authorization, transparency, accountability, trust, privacy, and safety.
  • Defined three compliance levels: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA.
  • Added foundation conformance expectations and maturity target labels.
  • Added the assessment planning model covering automated, semi-automated, and manual review methods.
  • Added governance requirements for identity, authorization, agent access, privacy, risk, trust, incident handling, audit, and policy management.
  • Added standard governance, versioning, normative content, informative content, glossary, future directions, and conclusion sections.
  • Clarified AWGS purpose text to say AWGS 1.0 provides a foundation for future measurable conformance criteria.

Guidelines and Supporting Guidance

  • Added implementation guidance organized around AWGS principles, governance domains, maturity target language, and assessment considerations.
  • Added guidance surfaces for Agentic Accessibility vision material, standards navigation, research, global glossary access, and future work.
  • Clarified that AWGS 1.0 compliance levels are provisional maturity targets until future AWGS 1.1 requirements and success criteria exist.
  • Added planned knowledge base topics for agent-ready services, evidence collection, human oversight workflows, and agent identity with delegated authority.
  • Identified exploratory future AWGS directions including agent-to-agent governance, multi-agent systems, autonomous transaction governance, identity frameworks, federated trust networks, reputation systems, continuous compliance, and safety assurance research.
